# Prop Firm Comparison

**One-sentence thesis**: Prop firms' marketing language is nearly identical ("pass the challenge + get funded + 80–90% split"), but **real rules, fees, restrictions, platforms, and compliance** differ dramatically. A wrong choice means $500–5000 in entry fees vanished for nothing. This chapter provides a data-driven head-to-head comparison.

> **Data timestamp**: numbers below verified on **2026-04-21** against each prop firm's official site + industry reviews (WebFetch-tested). Prop firm rules change frequently (monthly, sometimes weekly) — verify the **current** rules on the firm's site before making a purchase decision.

## 2. Challenge-Phase Rules Compared

**This is where 85–93% of participants wash out**. Small parameter differences determine whether a strategy is even viable.

### 1-Step / 2-Step / 3-Step Models

| Prop Firm | Model | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **FTMO** | 2-Step | Challenge 30 days + Verification 60 days |
| **TopStep** | 1-Step | Trading Combine has no time limit (until you violate rules or hit target) |
| **FundedNext** | 1-Step / 2-Step (choice) | "Stellar" is 1-step; "Evaluation" is 2-step |
| **The5ers** | Instant / 1-Step / 2-Step | Multiple paths; "Instant" has no challenge (but higher fees) |
| **CryptoFundTrader** | 1-Step / 2-Step (choice) | |
| **Apex Trader Funding** | 1-Step | No time limit, unlimited retries |
| **Maven Trading** | 2-Step | Standard |
| **FundingPips** | 1-Step / 2-Step (choice) | |

**Practical takeaways**:
- **1-Step looks easier**, but rules are usually tighter (3–4% daily loss vs. 5% for 2-Step)
- **No time limit** (TopStep, Apex) favors conservative strategies
- **Hard time limit** (FTMO 30 days) forces you to be aggressive → higher failure rate

### Challenge ROI Math

Assume you pay $500 for a $100K challenge with a 10% target = $10,000 profit.

**Probabilities** (industry estimates):
- Pass Challenge phase 1: ~15–25%
- Pass phase 2 (2-Step): 40–60% (conditional on passing phase 1)
- From payment to first payout: ~5–10%

**Expected ROI (single Challenge)**:
```
Conceptual pass:
  10% × $10,000 profit × 80% split = $800 expected gain
  But this is "conditional on passing"
  
Real expectation:
  5% pass-to-payout × $800 = $40 expected
  - $500 cost
  = -$460 expected (loss)
```

**Even under the most generous assumptions, the expected value of a single Challenge is negative**. Prop firms rely on **repeat participants** (sunk cost + the urge to prove yourself).

## 7. Recommendations by Trader Profile

### If you trade FX / commodities / index swings

**Top pick**: FTMO or The5ers
- Strongest brand, most transparent payouts
- cTrader support (quant- / algo-friendly)
- Cost: Challenge fees 10–20% higher

**Best value**: FundedNext or Maven Trading
- Similar rules, 20–30% cheaper fees
- Risk: shorter brand history

### If you trade US futures (ES, NQ, GC, etc.)

**Top pick**: TopStep
- Inventor of the Trading Combine model
- 14 years of operating history
- The **monthly subscription** is economical for continuous traders

**Runner-up**: Apex Trader Funding
- Stricter rules (2% daily loss) but lower prices
- Explosive growth, but short history (founded 2022)

### If you trade crypto

**The only serious option**: CryptoFundTrader
- Direct Bybit integration = real crypto market
- 715+ crypto pairs
- Cost: trust is moderate (only 3–4 years in business)

### If you want to trade both FX + crypto

**Compromise**: FundedNext or The5ers
- Both offer crypto CFDs (not spot)
- **Crypto CFD ≠ real crypto trading** (see `../02-platforms/plus500.md`)

### US users (the complicated 2024–2026 period)

- After the MFF event in 2024, nearly every prop firm paused US service
- 2025-08: FTMO re-entered via OANDA (MT5 + CFTC license)
- The5ers restored partial US service in 2025
- **Actual choices are limited**: FTMO is the mainstream option

## 8. Red Flag Checklist

Red flags to check for any prop firm:

### ❌ Immediate disqualifier

1. **Does not publicly disclose cumulative payouts** (asks you to "trust our Trustpilot score" instead)
2. **Trustpilot rating flagged by Trustpilot itself** (CryptoFundTrader had this at one point)
3. **Promises a "100% pass rate"** or similar impossible marketing
4. **Requires "margin" / "insurance deposits"** rather than a single Challenge fee
5. **Sudden changes to trading rules** without notifying paid users
6. **Customer support response time > 48 hours**
7. **Heavy complaints in Discord / Telegram groups about blocked payouts**
8. **Anonymous founders / management** or **inconsistent identities**
9. **Registered in a high-risk jurisdiction** (Seychelles, Belize, Marshall Islands) **and** no other entity

### ⚠️ Proceed with caution

1. Operating for < 2 years (includes CryptoFundTrader, Maven, FundingPips)
2. Founder is doing their **first prop firm** (vs. having an FX broker / trading background)
3. Single-platform dependence (e.g., MT5 only)
4. Does not permit hedging / opposing positions
5. Vague definition of "Consistency rule"
6. Complicated or opaque split structure

### ⚠️ Firm-specific flags

- **FundingPips**: purged by MetaQuotes in 2024 — ongoing license risk
- **Maven Trading**: only 3 years operating, scaling too fast — proceed carefully
- **The5ers**: 2024 user complaints about unclear "Consistency rule" interpretations
- **FundedNext**: 2024 reports from India / Middle East customers of lengthening payout cycles
- **Apex**: 2024 rule tightening, some longtime users feel it's "getting stricter"

## 9. Historical Lessons: Prop Firms That Went Under

Two **must-know** failure cases:

### MyForexFunds (MFF) — 2023 CFTC lawsuit

- 2020–2023, the world's second-largest by user count
- 2023-08: CFTC filed suit alleging $310M in "fraud"
- **Core allegation**: funded accounts were **never hedged to external markets** → pure B-book fraud
- Shuttered in 2024, users unable to withdraw
- 2025-05: the CFTC investigation was questioned on procedural grounds by a special commissioner; charges partially dropped
- **Lesson**: even when a firm "looks legit," **the business model itself** can be the problem

### True Forex Funds (TFF) — 2024 MetaQuotes delisting

- Founded in Budapest in 2020, rapidly grew into the top 5
- 2024-02-06: MetaQuotes **abruptly revoked** its MT5 license
- **Reason**: used a third-party "equity sync" that violated MT5 TOS
- **$3–5M in revenue gone overnight**
- 2024-03: the company ceased operations
- **Lesson**: single-platform dependence = single point of failure

## 10. How to Choose: Decision Process

### Step 1: Decide the market

- What do you want to trade? **FX? Futures? Crypto? All of the above?**
- Map to the "Market / Instrument Comparison" table above

### Step 2: Decide the budget

- How much can you afford in Challenge fees × failed attempts?
- Recommended budget = **2–3× your acceptable loss** (to cover the average number of failed attempts)

### Step 3: Evaluate strategy fit

- What is your strategy's R:R ratio? Your holding time?
- Match it against the Challenge rules (daily loss / max DD / consistency rule)
- **A rule mismatch = guaranteed failure**

### Step 4: Check trustworthiness

- Priority: FTMO, TopStep (long history + transparent disclosures)
- Runner-ups: The5ers, FundedNext, Apex
- Caution: emerging brands
- **Avoid**: firms that shut down / were sued / have anonymous management

### Step 5: Start small

- Begin with the **smallest account** ($10–25K); do not jump straight into $100K
- Pass the first one before considering scaling up
- **Do not** pay for several Challenges back-to-back (sunk cost trap)

### Step 6: Set exit rules

- Give yourself **no more than 3 attempts**
- 3 failures → pause for 90 days
- Still failing → consider changing strategy, not changing prop firm
