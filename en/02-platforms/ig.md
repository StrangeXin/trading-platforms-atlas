# IG Group (IG Markets)

## Overview

IG Group (LSE: IGG) is a UK-based global online trading provider founded in 1974 by Stuart Wheeler. It is the world's largest provider of CFDs and spread betting by client numbers and revenue, and the inventor of financial spread betting as a product category. IG is regulated by over 8 Tier-1 authorities globally including FCA (UK), ASIC (Australia), BaFin (Germany), MAS (Singapore), and CFTC (US via tastytrade). IG was promoted to the FTSE 100 in March 2026. The company sold its US retail derivatives brand Nadex to Crypto.com in 2022 for $216 million; in the same period, it had acquired tastytrade (US options broker) for $1 billion in 2020.

---

## History and Founding

**Stuart Wheeler** founded IG Index in 1974 with £30,000 from six friends. Having lost his job at First National Finance Corporation during the 1973 UK banking crisis, Wheeler created a spread betting product allowing investors to speculate on gold prices without buying the physical asset — and, crucially, without paying capital gains tax, since UK tax authorities treated spread betting as gambling rather than investment.

Key milestones:

- **1974**: IG Index founded; first financial spread bet offered on gold prices
- **1985**: First equity index spread bet offered on the FTSE 100
- **1990s**: Expansion into forex, bonds, and other CFD products
- **1998**: First online dealing interface developed — early move to electronic trading
- **2000**: IG Group Holdings formed as parent; lists on London Stock Exchange (LSE: IGG)
- **2003**: Full functional online trading platform launched; exponential client growth follows
- **~2000**: IG acquires HedgeStreet (US CFTC-regulated exchange); renames it Nadex (North American Derivatives Exchange)
- **2007**: L2 Dealer launched — IG's direct market access (DMA) platform for institutional-grade share trading with Level II market depth
- **2014**: ProRealTime advanced charting platform integrated; exclusively available through IG in the UK
- **2020**: IG acquires tastytrade (US options-focused retail brokerage) for $1 billion
- **December 2021**: IG Group agrees to sell Nadex and 39% stake in Small Exchange to Crypto.com for $216 million
- **2022**: Nadex sale to Crypto.com (Foris DAX Markets) completes; IG exits US retail binary options/limited risk derivatives brand
- **March 2026**: IG Group promoted to FTSE 100 index; 2024 financial year revenue of £1.04 billion

## Products and Services

### Spread Betting (UK/Ireland Only)

IG invented the product in 1974:
- Tax-free speculation on price movements under UK law (no CGT, no stamp duty)
- Legally classified as gambling in the UK, not investment; gains are therefore tax-free
- Available for stocks, indices, forex, commodities, rates, bonds
- Position sized in £ per point of movement; does not involve asset ownership
- Losses can exceed deposits; margin product with negative balance protection under FCA rules
- Exclusively available to UK and Irish retail clients

### CFD Trading (Global)

Primary product for non-UK clients:
- Contracts for Difference on 17,000+ underlying instruments
- Economically equivalent to spread betting but fully taxable in most jurisdictions
- Subject to leverage limits under ESMA (EU: 30:1 on major forex; 20:1 on major indices) and equivalent rules
- Not available to US retail clients (CFDs are not legal for retail US investors under CFTC/SEC rules)

### Share Trading (UK)

- Direct ownership of underlying shares (not CFDs)
- ISA and SIPP wrapper available for UK clients
- DMA access via L2 Dealer for institutional-level exchange execution

### tastytrade (US — acquired 2020)

- US-registered options and futures brokerage
- Separate brand and corporate identity; operates independently
- Focused on retail options traders; known for educational content (tastytrade content platform)
- Allows IG to have US retail presence under CFTC/SEC regulation without naming conflicts

### Nadex (Sold to Crypto.com, 2022)

- North American Derivatives Exchange
- CFTC-regulated binary options and limited risk derivatives exchange
- Acquired by IG from HedgeStreet (~2000)
- Unique product structure: fixed-risk binary options legal for US retail investors
- Sold to Crypto.com (Foris DAX Markets) for $216 million; also sold 39% stake in Small Exchange
- IG Group completed the sale in November 2024 (per AJ Bell reporting)

## Trading Platforms

IG provides four distinct platform options:

### IG Web Platform (Proprietary)

IG's primary browser-based platform:
- Full 17,000+ instrument universe
- Advanced charting with 100+ indicators
- Integrated news, economic calendar, and research
- Customizable layouts and watchlists
- Available on desktop and mobile web (no installation)
- Continuously updated by IG's internal engineering team

### L2 Dealer

IG's direct market access (DMA) desktop application:
- Connects directly to underlying exchange order books for share trading
- Level II market depth visible — full bid/ask ladder at each price level
- Used by active traders and institutions who want true exchange pricing, not CFD pricing over it
- Available for LSE, NYSE, NASDAQ, and other major equity exchanges
- Commission-based pricing (not spread)
- Targeted at professional and high-volume retail traders

### ProRealTime

Third-party charting and automated trading platform, exclusively available through IG in the UK:
- ~100 technical indicators and drawing tools
- Automated strategy backtesting and live execution engine
- ProOrder: rule-based automated trading module (no coding for basic strategies; PRT language for advanced)
- Separate subscription fee (~£30/month; waived if sufficient trading activity)
- Regarded as one of the more capable automated trading platforms available to retail clients

### MetaTrader 4 (MT4)

Available alongside IG's proprietary platforms:
- Targets traders who prefer MT4's familiar interface
- Supports Expert Advisors (EAs) for automated trading
- Limited to specific markets (primarily forex on IG)
- MT5 availability on IG varies by region; not consistently offered across all IG entities

## API

IG offers a public REST and WebSocket API:
- REST API: account data, order management, market data
- WebSocket Streaming API: real-time prices, account updates
- Documented for retail developers; OAuth-based authentication
- No FIX API for retail (institutional-grade FIX typically via tastytrade or direct arrangement)
- Used by third-party trading tools and algorithmic traders
