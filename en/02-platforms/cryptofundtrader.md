# CryptoFundTrader (CFT)

## Overview

CryptoFundTrader (CFT) is a cryptocurrency-focused proprietary trading firm that provides evaluation challenges and funded accounts for crypto traders. It distinguishes itself from the majority of prop firms by focusing specifically on cryptocurrency markets (rather than forex/CFDs), offering access to over 715 crypto pairs through a Bybit integration. The firm was founded approximately 2021–2022, and is legally registered as SWISS RLCRATES AG, headquartered in Zug, Switzerland. The CEO is Alan Sanchez. CFT offers funded accounts up to $300,000.

## Why "Crypto-Focused"

Most prop firms (FTMO, FundedNext, The5ers) offer some crypto CFDs, but their primary instrument base is forex, equity indices, and commodities. CFT is built specifically for crypto traders:

- **715+ crypto pairs** — more than any other prop firm by stated claim
- **Bybit as direct execution venue** — not a broker simulation but actual Bybit spot and derivatives
- **Leverage up to 1:100** on crypto instruments
- **900+ total instruments** including forex, indices, and commodities on MT5 platform

## Platform Options

CFT offers three platform paths:

### MetaTrader 5 (MT5)
- Standard CFD/forex simulation environment with added crypto pairs
- Spreads starting from 0 pips
- Full access to CFT's non-Bybit instrument range (forex, indices, commodities)
- Familiar interface for traders coming from forex prop firm backgrounds

### Match-Trader
- Proprietary platform technology used by multiple prop firms post-MetaQuotes enforcement
- CFT added Match-Trader as an MT5 alternative
- Web-based with modern UI

### Bybit (Direct Integration)

CFT's flagship offering and primary differentiator:
- Traders execute on the actual Bybit exchange terminal
- Access to Bybit's live liquidity for crypto perpetuals and spot
- 700+ crypto trading pairs — the largest selection among prop firms
- Real-time Bybit order book and price discovery
- Better crypto execution quality vs. a CFD simulation layer
- CFT manages risk and drawdown limits externally via account monitoring

The Bybit partnership is described by CFT as "redefining the future of crypto prop trading" — giving traders access to a real crypto exchange rather than a synthetic simulation environment.

## Challenge Structure

CFT uses a standard evaluation model with two stages:

### Stage 1 (Evaluation)
- Profit target: typically 8–10% of account size
- Daily drawdown: 3–5% (varies by account tier)
- Maximum drawdown: 5–10% (varies by tier)
- Time limit: none specified (as of 2024 research)

### Stage 2 (Verification)
- Lower profit target (5%)
- Same risk parameters as Stage 1
- Passed traders proceed to funded accounts

### Funded Accounts
- Account sizes: $5,000 to $300,000
- Profit split: typically 80%+ for funded traders
- Withdrawals: typically bi-weekly or monthly
- Scaling plan: funded accounts can scale based on performance

Specific parameters may change; always verify against official CFT documentation.

## Regulatory and Legal Considerations

- **Entity**: SWISS RLCRATES AG is a Swiss registered company; Zug is a prominent Swiss corporate registration jurisdiction for fintech and crypto companies
- **Regulation**: CFT does not claim to be a regulated financial services firm (prop firms generally operate outside retail financial services regulation as they are trading their own capital via simulated evaluation)
- **Bybit partnership**: Trading occurs on Bybit's regulated (Dubai VARA) or unregulated international infrastructure depending on the user's Bybit account type
- **US access**: As of 2024, CFT's availability to US users is not fully confirmed; many crypto prop firms restrict US clients due to the regulatory environment
