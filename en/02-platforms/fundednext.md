# FundedNext

## Overview

FundedNext is a proprietary trading firm founded in March 2022 by Syed Abdullah Jayed — a Bangladeshi entrepreneur — and is headquartered in Ajman, United Arab Emirates, with operational hubs in Bangladesh, UAE, Cyprus, Sri Lanka, and Malaysia. It is one of the fastest-growing prop firms globally, having distributed over $261 million in payouts to more than 93,000 traders by 2025. FundedNext operates an aggressive affiliate marketing model and supports four trading platforms: MT4, MT5, cTrader, and Match-Trader. The firm holds main-level MetaQuotes licenses for both MT4 and MT5 — unusual in the prop firm industry.

---

## History and Founding

**Syed Abdullah Jayed** is the founder of FundedNext and the parent company NEXT Ventures, a digital venture builder and fintech group operating from the UAE and Bangladesh. At the time of founding FundedNext, Jayed was an undergraduate student at North South University in Bangladesh — making him one of the youngest founders in the prop firm industry.

Key milestones:

- **March 2022**: FundedNext launched; initial offering based on standard two-stage challenge model
- **2022 (H2)**: Rapid growth fueled by aggressive affiliate program and social media marketing; particularly strong uptake in South/Southeast Asia, Middle East, and Africa
- **Early 2023**: FundedNext reaches significant scale; becomes one of the larger prop firms by challenge volume
- **Early 2024**: MetaQuotes enforcement actions across the prop industry; firms without proper MT4/MT5 licenses face disruption. FundedNext is able to continue MT4/MT5 operations citing its main-level licenses
- **February 2024**: FundedNext halts CFD prop trading for US traders following regulatory pressure (MetaQuotes and US regulatory landscape)
- **2024**: FundedNext adds Match-Trader as a fourth platform; cTrader integration also expanded
- **2024**: Affiliate program reaches maturity; up to 18% commission for referrals; described as one of the most competitive in the industry
- **2024–2025**: FundedNext re-enters US market with a futures prop platform (separate from its CFD offering)
- **2025**: Total payouts exceed $261 million to 93,000+ traders
- **2025**: Operations in UAE, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Cyprus, Malaysia confirmed; team described at 220K+ traders supported in 170+ countries

## Products and Challenges

FundedNext offers three primary challenge models:

### 1. Stellar (Two-Phase)

Standard two-stage evaluation:
- **Phase 1**: 8–10% profit target; 5–8% max drawdown; 30 trading days
- **Phase 2**: 5% profit target; same drawdown limits; 60 days
- **Funded**: 80%+ profit split; up to $4,000,000 account
- Account sizes: $6,000 to $200,000 (scales to $4M)

### 2. Stellar Lite (One-Phase)

Single-stage evaluation:
- Combined 10% profit target in one phase
- Faster path to funding for confident traders
- Same risk rules; typically 45 days

### 3. Express

Instant-style model:
- Higher fee; lower profit target in evaluation
- Designed for rapid onboarding

### Funded Account

- **Profit split**: 80% (can scale up to 95%)
- **Scaling**: Account doubles at each milestone; path to $4M maximum
- **Withdrawals**: Available after first 14 days of funded trading; bi-weekly thereafter
- **Trailing drawdown**: Applied in some models

### MetaQuotes License Distinction

FundedNext publicly states it holds **main-level MT4 and MT5 licenses** directly from MetaQuotes — not white-label sub-licenses from a third party. This is notable because the MetaQuotes enforcement actions in 2023–2024 targeted firms operating unlicensed or sub-licensed MT4/MT5 deployments. FundedNext's direct license means it was not affected by the enforcement that disrupted many peer firms.

Per [TradingView/Finance Magnates reporting](https://www.tradingview.com/news/financemagnates:f8c595249094b:0-prop-trading-firms-renaissance-cutting-us-clients-integrating-new-trading-platforms/): In early 2024, the MetaQuotes situation created urgency across the prop trading industry, with firms scrambling for alternatives. FundedNext was in a position to continue MT4/MT5 while also adding Match-Trader as an alternative.

### Match-Trader Integration

Match-Trade Technologies' Match-Trader platform emerged as the primary alternative for prop firms needing MT4/MT5 replacements in 2024:
- Web-based modern trading interface
- Native prop firm risk management tools
- FundedNext is a confirmed Match-Trader client with public testimonial on Match-Trade's website
- Match-Trader went from ~10 prop firm clients to ~60 in under a year (per PropFirmApp research)

## Affiliate / Partner Model

FundedNext's growth is substantially driven by an aggressive affiliate program:

- **Commission**: Up to 18% on referred challenge purchases
- **Structure**: Unique referral links; commissions on initial purchase and potentially recurring resets
- **Partner tiers**: Higher commission tiers for high-volume affiliates
- **Marketing**: FundedNext provides affiliates with marketing assets, banners, and discount codes
- **Impact**: The affiliate model is a primary growth vector; FundedNext has a large network of YouTube and social media creators promoting it

This model is similar to how FTMO grew in the 2018–2021 period but at a faster pace due to the matured social media ecosystem for prop firm content.

## US Market History

FundedNext's US operations have been complex:

- **Original US operations**: Available via MT5/MT4 CFD platform
- **February 2024**: US CFD prop trading halted — reasons cited include MetaQuotes' US policy (MetaQuotes does not license MT4/MT5 for certain US configurations) and broader regulatory caution
- **Re-entry**: FundedNext relaunched US market access via a **futures prop platform** (separate from CFD operations) — this uses NinjaTrader or similar futures infrastructure rather than MT5, navigating around MetaQuotes' US restrictions
- Per [FXVerify reporting](https://fxverify.com/news/exclusive-fundednext-re-enters-us-with-new-futures-prop-platform-7959): FundedNext re-entered US with a new futures prop platform
