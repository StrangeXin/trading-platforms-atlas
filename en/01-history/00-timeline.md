# Electronic Trading Timeline: 1970s–2026

A chronological record of major events in electronic trading, retail FX, and crypto markets. Each entry includes a date and a one-liner description.

---

## 1970s — Foundations

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1971-02-08 | **NASDAQ launches** as the world's first electronic stock market; initially a computerized bulletin board updating prices once a day, with orders still taken by phone. |
| 1976 | **NYSE's DOT (Designated Order Turnaround)** goes live, enabling electronic routing of small stock orders to specialists on the floor. |

## 1980s — Infrastructure Matures

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1982 | **Timber Hill Inc.** (predecessor to Interactive Brokers) begins coding computerized stock index futures and options pricing systems. Thomas Peterffy's first handheld trading computers appear on AMEX floor in 1983. |
| 1984 | **NASDAQ SOES** (Small Order Execution System) introduced, allowing electronic execution of small retail orders for the first time. |
| 1984 | **NYSE SuperDOT** replaces DOT; handles up to 100,000 shares per transaction, expanding capacity by 1,000×. |
| 1986-10-27 | **UK "Big Bang"** deregulation abolishes fixed commissions, opens London Stock Exchange to foreign firms, and triggers a rapid push toward electronic trading in European equities. |
| 1988 | **NASDAQ SOES mandatory** for all market makers following the 1987 crash, forcing electronic participation. |

## 1990s — Electronic Markets Take Hold

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1990 | **NASDAQ SelectNet** introduced, supporting large-block electronic trading beyond SOES size limits. |
| 1992 | **FIX Protocol 1.0** created by Robert Lamoureux and Chris Morstatt at Fidelity Investments and Salomon Brothers; first electronic machine-readable equity order protocol. |
| 1993 | **Interactive Brokers LLC** incorporated as a U.S. broker-dealer, extending Timber Hill's algorithmic market-making infrastructure to external clients. |
| 1996 | **OANDA Corporation** founded in Vancouver/New York; one of the first internet-era FX brokers to offer real-time currency data and eventually retail spot FX trading. |
| 1997 | **REDIBook ECN** launches (later absorbed by NYSE), bringing after-hours electronic equity trading to retail and institutional customers. |
| 1998 | **SEC Regulation ATS** allows ECNs to register as trading systems, legitimizing electronic venues outside traditional exchanges. |
| 1999 | **Archipelago (ArcaEx)** and **Island ECN** emerge as major electronic equity venues, foreshadowing full exchange competition. |
| 1999 | **FXCM** founded, among the first wave of internet-era retail FX brokers offering online trading to individuals. |

## 2000s — Retail FX Boom & Algorithmic Trading

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2000 | **MetaQuotes Software** founded in Kazan, Russia by Renat Fatkhullin; releases FX Charts, an early web-based charting tool for retail FX. |
| 2001 | **FIX 4.2** released; becomes the de facto standard still in widespread use decades later. |
| 2002 | **MetaTrader 3** released by MetaQuotes, adding futures support and introducing the MQL II algorithmic scripting language. |
| 2003 | **MetaTrader mobile** (CE and Palm) versions released; MetaQuotes pioneers mobile trading. |
| 2005-07-01 | **MetaTrader 4 (MT4) launches**; completely redesigned architecture with MQL4, Expert Advisors, and Strategy Tester. MT4 becomes the dominant retail FX platform for the next 15+ years. |
| 2006 | **FIX Trading Community** formalized as a non-profit standards body to govern FIX Protocol evolution. |
| 2007 | **eToro** founded in Tel Aviv as RetailFX by Yoni Assia, Ronen Assia, and David Ring; introduces social/copy trading to retail FX in 2010. |
| 2007-07-09 | **NYSE Euronext merger** completes, creating the first transatlantic exchange group; signals consolidation of major electronic markets. |
| 2008 | **Plus500** founded; 100% online CFD platform launches in Israel, expanding to Europe with no human brokers. |
| 2009-01-03 | **Bitcoin genesis block** mined by Satoshi Nakamoto; the Bitcoin network begins operation. |

## 2010s — High Frequency, Crypto, & Prop Firms

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2010-05-06 | **Flash Crash**: DJIA drops ~1,000 points in minutes due to HFT feedback loops; sparks global debate on algorithmic market structure. |
| 2010-06-01 | **MetaTrader 5 (MT5) launches**; multi-asset platform supporting Forex, stocks, and futures with MQL5 and improved backtesting. |
| 2010-07-18 | **Mt. Gox** begins exchange operations for Bitcoin (originally a Magic: The Gathering card trading site). |
| 2010 | **U.S. CFTC** limits retail FX leverage to 50:1 (major pairs) and 20:1 (others), reshaping the U.S. retail FX landscape. |
| 2011-04 | **cTrader** launched by Spotware Systems (Cyprus); FxPro becomes the first broker to offer it. |
| 2011 | **TradingView** founded (incorporated 2011); early cloud-based charting platform selected by TechStars in 2013. |
| 2012 | **IEX** (Investors Exchange) founded by Brad Katsuyama to counter predatory HFT; popularized by Michael Lewis's *Flash Boys* (2014). |
| 2013 | **TradingView** signs data contracts with CME Group and Microsoft during TechStars program; begins its growth toward mass-market charting. |
| 2013-04 | **Bitcoin surpasses $200** for first time; Mt. Gox handles 70%+ of global BTC volume. |
| 2014-02-07 | **Mt. Gox halts Bitcoin withdrawals**, later files bankruptcy; reveals 850,000 BTC (~$473M) lost. Triggers first major crypto-exchange regulation (Japan). |
| 2015 | **FTMO** founded in Prague, Czech Republic by Otakar Šuffner and Marek Vašíček; pioneers the "funded trader challenge" model. |
| 2016 | **Nasdaq acquires ISE** and completes purchase of Chi-X Canada; exchange consolidation wave continues. |
| 2017-07 | **Binance** founded by Changpeng Zhao (CZ) following a $15M ICO; reaches #1 global crypto exchange by volume within 6 months. |
| 2017 | **ICO boom**: Ethereum-based initial coin offerings raise billions globally; the SEC issues a warning in July; market cap peaks above $800B in early 2018. |
| 2018-05 | **TradingView** raises $37M Series A led by Insight Venture Partners; acquires TradeIT (broker order-routing middleware). |
| 2018 | **OANDA** acquired by private equity firm CVC Capital Partners; accelerates its digital platform investment. |
| 2019-09-05 | **BUSD launched** by Binance and Paxos; dollar-backed stablecoin anchors Binance's trading ecosystem. |
| 2019 | **Uniswap v1** launches on Ethereum mainnet; automated market maker (AMM) DEX model challenges centralized exchanges. |

## 2020s — Crypto Matures, Prop Firm Crackdown, Platform Wars

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2020 | **COVID-19 pandemic** drives retail trading boom globally; PFOF controversy surges; Robin Hood, eToro, and prop firms see unprecedented user growth. |
| 2021-10 | **TradingView** valued at $3 billion in a $298M funding round led by Tiger Global; becomes the dominant retail charting platform globally with millions of daily users. |
| 2021 | **FTX** at peak valuation of $32B; Sam Bankman-Fried among the most prominent crypto figures. |
| 2022-09-23 | **Apple removes MT4 and MT5** from the App Store globally; linked to Western sanctions on Russia given MetaQuotes' Russian origins. MetaQuotes denies sanctions connection. |
| 2023-03 | **Apple reinstates** MetaTrader 4 and MetaTrader 5 on the App Store after ~6 months. |
| 2022-11 | **FTX collapse**: ~$8B customer funds missing; Sam Bankman-Fried arrested; triggers global crypto regulatory scrutiny. |
| 2023-08 | **MyForexFunds (MFF) shut down**: CFTC and Ontario Securities Commission freeze assets of Toronto-based prop firm; allege $310M fraud. The case later collapses — CFTC ordered to pay $3.1M in attorney fees. |
| 2024-01-10 | **Bitcoin Spot ETF approved** by the SEC (BlackRock, Fidelity, etc.); marks institutional legitimization of BTC. |
| 2024-02-06 | **True Forex Funds** loses MT4/MT5 license without warning from MetaQuotes; first visible casualty of the MetaQuotes prop-firm crackdown. CEO Richard Nagy: "MetaQuotes did not give any prior warning." |
| 2024-02-11 | **Purple Trading** terminates multiple prop firms (Funded Engineer, AquaFunded, Goat Funded Trader, The Funded Trader, Skilled Funded Trades) under MetaQuotes pressure. |
| 2024-02-14 | **Blackbull Markets** terminates Funding Pips; CEO Anish Lal confirms: "We're forced to immediately shut down Funding Pips as a client." MetaQuotes crackdown enters public view. |
| 2024 | **~80–100 prop firms close** globally; MetaTrader's prop-firm market share drops from 48% to 24% within 9 months. DxTrade, MatchTrader, TradeLocker, cTrader benefit. |
| 2024-01 | **Oleg Mukhanov** appointed CEO of TradingView; over 100 brokers now directly integrated. |
| 2025-Q1 | **MetaQuotes halts all new MT4 licenses**; signals MT4 end-of-life path. Existing brokers retain access. |
| 2025-07-01 | **MetaQuotes ends support** for older MT4 and MT5 builds; minimum build requirements enforce platform updates. |
| 2025-12-01 | **FTMO completes acquisition of OANDA** from CVC; first major prop firm to own a regulated multi-jurisdiction broker. Deal secured 5 regulatory approvals across 8 months. |
| 2026 | **Prop firm regulatory pressure intensifies**: multiple jurisdictions (UK FCA, EU ESMA, US CFTC) examining whether funded challenges constitute regulated financial activity. |

---

## Key Themes

1. **Democratization curve**: Exchange floor → ECNs → retail internet brokers → social trading → prop firm challenges → AI-scored signals.
2. **Platform consolidation then disruption**: MT4 dominated retail FX for 15+ years; TradingView and alternative platforms eroding that moat.
3. **Regulatory lag**: Each innovation (HFT, retail FX, crypto, prop challenges) runs 5–10 years ahead of clear regulation.
4. **Crypto–TradFi convergence**: Bitcoin ETFs, prop firms buying regulated brokers (FTMO/OANDA), exchanges adding fiat on/off ramps.
